# Enterprise Rollout Model

## Purpose

This model describes future maturity levels for enterprise adoption of the AI-SDLC framework.

It is a progression model, not a mandatory deployment plan.

## Maturity Levels

### Level 0 - Traditional SDLC

- Entry criteria: standard software delivery practices
- Success criteria: stable delivery with defined ownership
- KPIs: delivery predictability, defect rate, lead time

### Level 1 - AI Assisted

- Entry criteria: team can safely use AI for isolated tasks
- Success criteria: improved productivity without governance loss
- KPIs: assisted task throughput, review quality, adoption rate

### Level 2 - Spec Driven

- Entry criteria: approved intent and specification discipline exists
- Success criteria: delivery follows structured requirements
- KPIs: spec coverage, traceability coverage, rework reduction

### Level 3 - Governed AI Delivery

- Entry criteria: governance, approvals, and traceability are operating
- Success criteria: AI-assisted delivery remains reviewable and auditable
- KPIs: approval compliance, validation pass rate, context freshness

### Level 4 - AI Native Delivery

- Entry criteria: AI is embedded across the delivery lifecycle
- Success criteria: teams consistently use governed AI patterns end to end
- KPIs: cycle time, quality, evidence completeness, context reuse

### Level 5 - Autonomous Delivery

- Entry criteria: strong governance, high confidence automation, and mature observability
- Success criteria: safe autonomous execution within explicit bounds
- KPIs: autonomy rate, exception rate, incident rate, control adherence

## Notes

- Movement between levels should be evidence-based.
- Different squads may sit at different maturity levels at the same time.
